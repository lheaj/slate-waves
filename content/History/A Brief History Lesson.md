As recounted by Sir Le-Boubouche:

“Not that long ago, in a world now buried under the waves, great continents were wiped clean of their histories, settlements, and peoples. Blabbers charlatans and clerics whisper of a cataclysmic deluge from the heavens, a divine punishment that shattered stone and wood, drowning entire civilizations. Jibber-jabber. [Cataclysm](History/Cataclysm.md) it was, but if you ask me there was nothing unscientific about it, just darn luck evolution wanted us to boot-off the face of this world.

Forests, mountains, deserts, and cities all vanished beneath relentless torrents, falling from a dark, sunless sky. The rains reshaped the world into a patchwork of isolated islands, once towering peaks, now adrift without their histories or resources.

Life on these islands was slow to start, until the discovery of [CO7](CO7.md). There our luck changed. A small band of scientists received signals hinting at dormant kinetic energy deep in the oceans. It took two years of daring underwater expeditions, using newly designed submarine suits, to reach these depths. I tell you, my grandfather lost a fortune in those, and his “explorers” only brought back empty tins and the ordinary cadaver. What are we to do with a dead body? You’d think a darn paid explorer would think about it before hauling it up? Anyway, I digress. A lucky group returned with nets heavy with seemingly fragile black stones. Days later, an explosion in their lab-shack confirmed it: they had found fuel. A guy call Derrick was burned alive in the process, but hey, all in the name of science, isn’t it?

They named it Colanovo Operatium 7 Kinetic Romboron Satista Terranium Maraudus 93, or more simply, CO7krstm93— but even that was long, so people started calling it just CO7. A mere shock could ignite the energy within these stones, producing explosions powerful enough to start fires, power machines, or even pierce through living targets. Thus began the age of industrialization. Pew Pew.

With CO7, small civilizations flourished on the secluded islands. Survivors of the rains mixed their residual knowledge, constructing new towns from the ruins of the old, powered by this new dark fuel. Engineers became the new clerics, placing their faith in the physics of this new world. Wizards, stripped of their books, had to relearn magic from scratch, some venturing into its darkest corners. Not worth it if you ask me, those cuckoos summon who-knows-what to cast a fireball? Give me a couple of CO7 stones and I’ll show you how it’s done without selling a chunk of my soul!

Anyway. Elves, dwarves, humans, orcs, halflings, and gnomes all worked together to build a new society. However, without a formal government, justice was harsh and simple. Ancestries too diverse to converge never agreed on a unified ruler. Instead, each island pooled funds to pay for public services, including a guard and [The Juridical System](Miscellaneous/The%20Juridical%20System.md). The rules were clear:

- Liars lose their tongues.
- Thieves lose a limb.
- Fraudsters lose all possessions.
- Killers face death.

Before this was in place I tell you the odd Elf murder at the hand of an angry shorty wasn’t at all that uncommon.

This straightforward legal system required no jails; private justice was often the safest solution. Every business hired its own contractors for protection and financial affairs. Beyond the rare, inhabited islands, the world turned cruel and lawless. Pirate ships, ranging from small sails to massive war machines, patrolled the seas, seeking easy prey. If you get out there, be cautious or they’ll strip you naked and then some.

Without formal governments, the true rulers were those with enough gold to buy power. In this world, gold is king. What started as a watery nightmare became an opportunity for many. To “rise from the ashes” they see. More like— climb on the back of an army of underpaid labour. In any case there was some truth in that. Every being, whether man, elf, dwarf, orc, bugbear, or troll, started equally, forging their destinies anew.

Occasionally, an islander dreams of a past filled with greenery, cultures, and friendships, only to wake to the intoxicating smell of [CO7](CO7.md) smelting, trapped in a world of endless waters.

But that’s not the case for all of you isn’t it. Some have just been thawed out from a frozen underwater natural cryogenic deposit that somehow preserved you from top to toes. What a luck! Well, yes, all your friends and family might be dead, but outside a great new life awaits! Thank the big brains of the scientists of [Smallport](Smallport.md) at work. Yes, yes, especially me.

Well, now that you know the story -more or less- you are ready to get out there and become a hero! Or a beggar, whatever suits you. Oh, before I forget! Here is your pocket change, as defined by the charity statute of the Sea-found. Will serve you for a couple of days, until you can start slaving for some of the richer folk!”
