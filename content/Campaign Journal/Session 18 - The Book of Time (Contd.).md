The dragons enveloped the adventurers into lighting blasts and breaths of fire that tested their endurance and strategic abilities. Ultimately, the group managed to vanquish the wyrmlings and free Arthur's family of silver dragons. 

Alas, seeking an exit from the [[Ethernal Plane]] was not going to be easy. The energy feeding the clock-tower from the silver dragons lifespan had been turned off and the portal back to the material plane closed. The group waited near the tower until they met [[Arthur Silverwing]] again. This time, the dragon had aged into its maturity. He recounted how time passed differently in the Ethernal Plane and how he was lost seeking their brothers for centuries, flying among ruins and desolate planes. He shared his knowledge on how to escape: face the ghost of past and present that live on the Etherenal Plane, as they are the only left with a real connection to the real timeline. Gosha thanked Arthur, who explained he was not able to attach himself to the same magic and would be prisoner of the Ethernal Plan until the Wizard who banished there was defeated. Gosha promised to free him.

The group then spoke to different visions, this time, setting aside their anger toward their past or visions of the future they didn't like, and accepting their responsibility toward the harm caused, often involuntary, by their actions.

The five woke up back at the [[Hotel Tusseaux]].
