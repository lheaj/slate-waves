Marlin Everstaff awoke from his brief nap, a deep crease marring his wrinkled brow. His aging mind faltered, struggling to recall basic functions—simple acts like walking or even spelling his own name seemed almost beyond him. Fortunately, Thom The Tuba, a teenager with an enormous heart but a limited reserve of courage, rushed to the elderly fairy’s side, offering help as best as he could.

What followed was nothing short of chaos: lightning streaked through the air, fire-breathing owls unleashed their fury, and combatants fired from behind a staircase, contorting themselves into poses almost too peculiar to describe. In the end, the group triumphed, overcoming the workers and the Tiefling Jo. She was now pinned against a metal door, her mechanical hands trapped in an icicle melded to the iron frame, courtesy of the wizard’s owl and its freezing breath.

Vanessa wasted no time. After a brisk interrogation punctuated by sharp slaps to [[Jo - The Brass Rat,]]'s face, she drew her knife and pressed it against the Tiefling's warm, organic neck. Leaning in close, she whispered with chilling calm, “Do you know why they call me the Vulture? It’s because, when everyone else is done, I claim the scraps for myself.” The blade slid slowly but unerringly into Jo's flesh, blood seeping over Vanessa’s hands as a satisfied grin spread across her face. Broken and bleeding, Jo confessed. Among the many horrifying truths she revealed, one stood out: her organization was responsible for abducting young test subjects and sending them to a wizard who performed sinister experiments in [[Watersafe]], the city beneath the ocean.

With this revelation, the group set the factory ablaze, using low-grade local explosives to leave a blazing trail of destruction in their wake. They fled through the labyrinth of Perison’s sewers, eventually returning to [[the Dreamers]]’ hideout. But what awaited them wasn’t the raucous welcome of rebel comrades—only deathly silence and a room littered with lifeless bodies. The witch’s army had discovered the hideout and decimated the insurrectionists.

Amid the carnage, Elias, the young thief, survived, though gravely wounded. Her voice trembled as she recounted the events: the massacre, the utter collapse of their forces, and the harrowing abductions of [[Ravesha]], Gosha’s sister, and [[Fanger]]. Gosha, the Genasi, sat on a bed where his sister had been only hours before, staring blankly at his hands. He absently rubbed the sheets, still damp and smelling faintly of the sea, grief etched into his features.

Exhausted and battered from their struggles, the group sought refuge in the ruins of an abandoned building, where they could rest and gather their strength. The following day, determined to press on, they set their sights on the witch’s childhood home, a destination they had previously discussed with [[Roderick]]. Their goal was clear: to learn more about their foe before attempting a daring rescue of their captured companions.

The house was a hollow echo of its past—a desolate, lifeless shell. Each step across the creaking floor resounded through cold, empty rooms. The walls, thin and dust-coated, reeked of mildew. Within this forsaken place, the group encountered a spectral presence: the ghost of Violet’s father. It was here they began to understand the tormented past that shaped the infamous witch of Perison.

Even in death, her father’s malevolence was undiminished. He spewed abhorrent words, steeped in racism, sexism, and casual violence, bragging of the punishments he inflicted on his disobedient daughter. But [[Violet - The Witch of Perison]] had not been defeated by him. She had mastered powerful, forbidden magics, binding her father’s soul to this house as an eternal prison. Without hesitation, the group made their decision. They left the mansion behind, allowing the wretched ghost to rot in his self-inflicted torment.

Now, their path was clear. It was time to confront the witch.
