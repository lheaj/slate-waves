Right after exiting Princess' room, Marlin conjured the illusion of a book thief, to deflect the blame from his "trade", seemingly convincing Regina. 

Back into their luxurious suite, driven by curiosity, the group got to investigate the tome. A functioning eye was etched into the cover, watching the real thieves as they tried to pry its secrets. After solving a number of riddles, the magic hidden within the parched paged revealed itself, porting everyone in the room into a different dimension into the [[Ethernal Plane]].

They opened their eyes to a clock-tower, erected into the nothingness of a starless space, standing on floating debris of what was once a stone castle.

Here, Arthur, a young silver dragon wyrmling member of the [[Silverwings]], was fighting mondrones, trying to escape captivity. After thanking the adventurers for their help, Arthur explained how [[The Wizard]] kidnapped him and his brothers, locked them in the clock-tower, and drained their lifeforce to feed his own immortality. Arthur also cautioned the group about the spirits of past and futures etched in the time web.

As they walked to the tower, the group met with different visions. Thom faced the ghost of his father and reckoned with his own anger. Marlin met his parents, accusing him of warping time and causing their deaths, while Thunderfoot was met with accusation of hypocrisy over his swatting of a fly.

The group then finally entered the tower, where they fought more visions and conjured dragon wyrmlings to free the Silverwings from their timeless imprisonment.
