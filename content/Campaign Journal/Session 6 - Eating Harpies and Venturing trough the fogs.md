Vanquishing a harpy might typically involve sword strikes, flying arrows, and flaming spells—all valid options, yet none as efficient as a Giant Frog's bite and swallow, followed by digestive acid and the casual burping of misshapen harpy feathers.

A half-deal was struck with one of the Goblin mercenaries, but once the green, snot-colored rascal demonstrated a clear lack of trustworthiness, Marco executed him on the spot. The ordeal caused a bit of a fissure between him and the all-so-perfect group of heroes, but love wasn’t in the air anyway.

Once the fuel was obtained, the group strolled back to the beach for one last chat with the ever-so-charming house-sized crab. The hermit dispensed his own trinkets and presents to the group, including a Wand of Smiles for the whimsical [[Marlin Everstaff]].

On the boat, the party had a chance to rest. Gosha once again experienced a seemingly foretelling dream, a mixture of darkness and hope, while Marlin continued working on new spells. A magical incident resulted in the ephemeral apparition of three young fairies with features similar to the crone mage. The party was left wondering ever more about the true nature of the seemingly genuine wizard.

The boat finally arrived at [[Perison Island]]. Its thick fogs engulfed the ship as it moved through the port, clashing with the souls of its passengers and making its presence felt within the morale of the group. The smoke enveloped parts of Gosha’s and Thom’s lungs and hearts, as if shielding their inner eyes from their own dreams and goals, obscuring parts of their very essence (and leaving them with disadvantage on Wisdom saving throws, of course).

Lying as a beggar on the brink of death, [[Ravesha]], [[Gosha]]’s sister, was waiting for her life to vanish among the streets of Perison. The young Genasi was as thrilled by the encounter as he was devastated and preoccupied with the sight of his family in such distress. His dreams and premonition became all too real in the span of a few moments.

Some short time later, [[Vanessa The Vulture]] got to use her new toy, the flying broom, to rescue a child from a collapsing building amid a rain of rocks naturally detaching from a shadowing ravine coasting the town. Helping a child in need is not really in her chords, but she performed brilliantly, nonetheless. Is there hope for anyone to turn good in the end? Perhaps a faint one for the Vulture, so thin it would probably drift away like pollen in the air at the first gust of wind.

She was about to be hit by another rolling boulder when a woman dressed in violet and clutching a black magic book shattered the rock into dust.

After a quick meeting with what they would come to understand was the [[Violet - The Witch of Perison]], the group directed themselves toward a town square where a group of guards, led by a towering Warforged, was announcing an increase in V.A.T. (Very Awful Taxes).

The tensions between the enforcers and the unhappy commoners simmered like a gnomish pepper stew at max heat. Will the group partake in what seems to be a fiery mess brewing ahead, or will they seek safety elsewhere?
