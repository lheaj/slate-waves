The Genasi was home if ever his people had one, but as he swam, he could only see and sense emptiness. The cocoons serving as homes, laced onto the gigantic reefs within the abyss, were silent. [Gosha](Characters/Gosha.md)’s heartbeat drummed his hear, accompanying him as he entered a familiar lair. He swam cautiously through what would have been considered a window from the people above waters. The reefs purple glow did not enter the cocoons, without it, darkness was even more impenetrable. He stepped calmly on the sandy floor, feeling the granules on his bare feet, stretching his toes. His right hand caressed the soft white walls as he steadied his balance, leaving salt on his palm.

“Where is everybody?” He asked no one.

“Dead.” No one replied.

Gosha stepped further toward the speaker, hesitating before detaching his hand from the wall. He floated.

Sands swirled as a shapeless creature shifted from its slumber.

“Above, a woman once asked me a question.”

The voice’s growl mixed with the Genasi’s heartbeat and echoed within the oval structure of the cocoon.

“I answered with truth, and she found it revolting. Yet her heart swam in love's boundless sea. She embraced the illusion she cherished and thus, met her end in blissful deception.”

The quiet hiss of a snake moving along a desert filled the room as the darkness circled around the Genasi as a predator closing in on its meal.

“Saddened, I harvested the incommensurate vastness of her sentiment. A gift for my people not to perish again. Yet they died, as you see.”

A dot of emerald radiance shattered the blackness around Gosha’s eyes. As a mouse hugged by an anaconda, he floated encircled by an endless scaly tail, made to digest men with ease. Whiskers drifted from a leonine muzzle the size of an elephant’s head.

“Little fish.” It roared quietly. “What do you desire?”

The emerald light exploded into a blinding brightness.

Gosha’s eyes opened calmly to Smallport sunshine timidly peering through the poorly closed curtains.
