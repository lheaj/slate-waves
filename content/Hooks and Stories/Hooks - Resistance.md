The deafening explosion shattered the stone wall [[Roderick]] was leaning on, propelling the man 15 feet forward. A weightless rag doll, he flew past a mining cart, ears aflame with a high pitch ringing. He thudded on the ground and whirled uncontrollably, narrowly missing a jagged boulder a few inches from his head. The taste of iron and dust filled his mouth as he regained a blurry vision. A grey mass loomed over him, the sun barely visible through the haze.

The buzzing of a thousand bees exploded from a roaring chainsaw right above his head, as it spun, rusted blood and flesh sprayed on to his face. A mechanical monstrosity built of iron and apathy towered over him, a monotonic grey figure if not for a flapping purple scarf.  Its body was clad in a rudimental plate armour, heavy beyond what any human could have worn, greyed with smoke and smouldering with the heat of battle. The veteran’s instinct kicked-in, he rolled his shoulder to the side just as a thousand rotating dents bit the into the ground where he’d been. He pushed at the iron giant’s leg and duck under it, gaining some distance. He searched for weaknesses but there was no opening on its junctures. Knees and elbows were covered with a slash resistant black gum and its helmet barely left a slit open for a pair of gleaming red inhuman eyes to seek its target.

[Roderick](Actors/Roderick.md) flew three steps back and looked around. His brigade was still fighting over the human soldiers draped in purple. Wyatt and Colton, his best men where exchanging hammer blows with two guards in the service of [Violet - The Witch of Perison](Actors/Violet%20-%20The%20Witch%20of%20Perison.md). Their adversaries seemed clumsy and inexperienced in comparison. He jumped behind another black stone boulder to gain a few seconds of coverage. Cheyenne and her long-range gun were hanging over the partly wrecked tower where he was standing just a few moments ago. She was crouched, unflinching, barrel resting on what was left of a wooden banister, as she delivered death to the witch’s minions one after the other. He tried to call in vain, his voice muffled by a constant explosions and battle cries. The grey, barren ground had only one gift for the people of [Perison Island](Places/Perison%20Island.md), rocks. Roderick picked a pebble without much consideration and hurled it toward the sniper. The rock dented a wooden pillar behind the sniper just as she pressed the trigger on another headshot. She did not turn. He grimaced, hurried back to the ground and lifted a larger fist-size rock. His throbbing ring finger was crocked in an unnatural way, but there wasn’t time to ponder over whether it was broken.

Roderick flung the hope filled stone toward his companion once more but wouldn’t get to see where it landed. The boulder he was using as cover burst into fragments racing through the air as bullets shot from a blunderbuss. He tried to cover his face with both hands as the stone projectiles pierced through his shoulders, legs and flank side to side. A single chip meticulously sliced through his hand to lodge itself a fraction of an inch from his left eye as he fell again back to the dust. The armoured terror stepped on what was left of the boulder, its weight crushing the remaining stone to dust. Slow and certain it moved, like old age and terminal sickness. Its deep and emotionless voice resounded clear above the fighting.

“Surrender. Madame Violet doesn’t wish for all this death.”

Roderick rose.

“We will never sleep through a colourless life; we will never let our wishes die.” He was now yelling.

Wyatt and Colton joined in.

“We will never raise obedient children; we will never let their souls be defeated.”

Bell, Lila, Zeke, Clodette, and tens others joined the chant biting words through their wounds.

“We will never forget our freedom quest; we will never let our enemies rest. We will fight today as we did in the past; and nothing will shatter our dreams to Perison’s dust.”

Cheyenne’s barrel filled with fire and electric sparks as a flat headed bullet pierced the air leaving behind a blue blinding flash. The projectile hit the iron cladded killer right in the back of the helm, its chainsaw jammed instantly as its body convulsed  engulfed by lightning rod filling the air with the smell of burnt oil.

Roderick yelled one more time as he lifted another nearby boulder, dust filled smoke and electricity encircling him. With both hands, he plunged stone into the stunned iron helm flattening it like an empty tin with the weight of his fury and never-dying hope.
