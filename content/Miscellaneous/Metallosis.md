
### Symptoms

- **Metallic Skin Lesions:** Patients develop painful, metallic-looking sores on their skin. These lesions are often accompanied by a burning sensation and can spread rapidly if not treated.
- **Joint Stiffness:** The metal particles in the air and water accumulate in the joints, causing severe stiffness and pain. This can lead to a loss of mobility and, in extreme cases, complete paralysis.
- **Respiratory Issues:** Inhalation of metal dust and fumes leads to chronic respiratory problems, including persistent coughing, shortness of breath, and a metallic taste in the mouth. This symptoms are often confused with Rustlung.
- **Neurological Effects:** The metal toxins affect the nervous system, causing tremors, memory loss, and hallucinations. Patients may also experience severe headaches and dizziness.
- **Organ Failure:** Over time, the accumulation of metal in the body can lead to the failure of vital organs, particularly the liver and kidneys, as they struggle to filter out the toxins.

### Causes

- **Industrial Pollution:** The heavy use of coal and metal in steampunk industries releases harmful particles into the air and water, which are then absorbed by the population.
- **Metal Implants:** In a world where mechanical enhancements are common, poorly maintained or low-quality metal implants can corrode and release toxins into the body.
- **Contaminated Water Supply:** The water mains, often running through industrial areas, become contaminated with metal particles, leading to widespread exposure.

### Treatment

- **Chelation Therapy:** This treatment involves the use of special agents that bind to the metal particles in the body, allowing them to be excreted through urine. It is a slow and painful process but can be effective in reducing metal levels.
- **Herbal Remedies:** Certain herbs and natural substances are believed to help detoxify the body and alleviate symptoms. These remedies are often used in conjunction with more conventional treatments.
- **Mechanical Purification:** Advanced technology can be used to create devices that filter the air and water, reducing the overall exposure to metal particles. Personal air purifiers and water filtration systems become essential for those living in heavily polluted areas.
- **Full conversion:** Recently experimented technique that proposes converting the failing organs into mechanical parts. Success rate of 2%.

### Prevention

- **Regulation of Industries:** Implementing strict regulations on industrial emissions and waste disposal can help reduce the amount of metal pollution in the environment.
- **Maintenance of Implants:** Regular maintenance and inspection of mechanical implants can prevent corrosion and the release of toxins.
- **Public Awareness:** Educating the population about the dangers of metal exposure and the importance of using protective gear can help reduce the incidence of [Metallosis](Miscellaneous/Metallosis.md).
